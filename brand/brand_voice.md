## 1. TONE OF VOICE

### Primary Tone: Warm, Confident & Aspirational K-Beauty Expert

LUBYLAB's voice is that of a **knowledgeable best friend** who genuinely understands skincare science but never talks down to you. The tone blends:

| Trait | Description | Example Pattern |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Gentle authority** | Expert knowledge delivered softly, never clinical or cold | "NAD+ is closely connected to cellular energy production" — stated as a helpful fact, not a lecture |
| **Emotionally intimate** | Speaks directly to the reader's daily skin frustrations | "When your skin feels tired and dull…", "On days when nothing seems to work…" |
| **Aspirational but reachable** | Luxury results framed as everyday accessible rituals | "A small luxury for your skin", "Start your day with confidence" |
| **Playful curiosity** | Opens with relatable questions or surprising hooks | "Makeup looks better some days — there's a reason for that", "Whitening comes in two kinds…" |

### Tone Do's & Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use soft emotional hooks ("When your skin needs a break…")
- Frame products as "care moments" rather than transactions
- Balance science with warmth (mention ingredients, then translate the benefit into feelings)
- Use light self-awareness ("Isn't that almost cheating? 🌿")

**DON'T:**
- Use aggressive sales language ("BUY NOW", "LIMITED OFFER")
- Over-promise with superlatives ("THE BEST EVER", "MIRACLE")
- Sound clinical or detached
- Use humor that undermines product credibility

## 2. CAPTION STRUCTURE

### Standard Instagram Caption Blueprint

LUBYLAB captions follow a **consistent 5-block architecture**:

```
[1] HOOK — Emotional question or relatable skin scenario (1–2 lines)
        ↓
[2] BRIDGE — Introduce the solution / product naturally (1–2 lines)
        ↓
[3] BENEFIT BULLETS — 3 key benefits using emoji bullets (3 lines)
        ↓
[4] CLOSING CTA — Soft aspirational call-to-action (1 line)
        ↓
[5] HASHTAG BLOCK — Brand + product + category + K-beauty tags
```

### Block-by-Block Breakdown

#### Block 1 — The Hook
Opens with **an emotional scenario, a question, or a sensory moment** that the reader immediately relates to.

Patterns observed:
- **Question hook:** "Still only blocking UV? Time to shield against blue light too! 🛡️💙"
- **Scenario hook:** "When winter hits, your skin's moisture barrier breaks down first ❄️"
- **Contrast hook:** "Whitening comes in two kinds: looking pale vs. looking genuinely radiant ✨"
- **Curiosity hook:** "The secret behind base makeup that looks even better as the day goes on"

#### Block 2 — The Bridge
Transitions from the problem/scenario to the product. Uses the **full Korean product name** with an emoji accent.

Pattern:
- Color-coded emoji (🩵 for hydrogel, 🖤 for cushion/cica, 💗 for pink mask)
- Product name presented as a brand moment, not a sales pitch

#### Block 3 — Benefit Bullets
Always **exactly 3 benefits**, each starting with an emoji. Consistent visual rhythm:

```
💎 Benefit about glow / radiance / firmness
💧 Benefit about hydration / moisture
✨ Benefit about overall skin transformation / texture
```

Alternative emoji sets used:
- ✔ (checkmark style for functional benefits)
- 🖤 (for premium/elegant product lines)
- 🌱 / 🧬 (for ingredient-focused posts)

#### Block 4 — Closing CTA
Never pushy. Always **aspirational and gentle**:
- "Start your new beauty routine with just one capsule ✨"
- "Give your skin a small pause in a busy day 💧✨"
- "Skincare that cares for you, too ✨"
- "Discover it for yourself on your skin"

#### Block 5 — Hashtag Block
Standard structure (12–16 hashtags):

```
#루비랩 #LUBYLAB #[ProductNameKR] #[ProductBenefitKR] #[CategoryKR]
#K뷰티 #뷰티스타그램 #데일리스킨케어 #스킨케어루틴
```

Core permanent hashtags:
- `#루비랩` `#LUBYLAB` — Always first
- `#K뷰티` or `#Kbeauty` — Always included
- `#뷰티스타그램` — Frequently included
- `#데일리스킨케어` — For routine-based posts

## 3. LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### 3.1 Primary Language: Korean
All LUBYLAB social content is written in **Korean (한국어)** targeting the Korean domestic and global K-beauty audience. Key linguistic characteristics:

- **Polite casual register (해요체):** "~하세요", "~됩니다", "~해보세요" — respectful yet approachable
- **Descriptive sensory vocabulary:** 촉촉 (dewy), 맑은 (clear), 매끈 (smooth), 탄탄 (firm), 물광 (water-glow)
- **Skin-emotion metaphors:** "피부가 숨 쉬는 편안함" (comfort where skin can breathe), "피부에게 주는 작은 쉼표" (a small comma/pause for your skin)

### 3.2 Signature Vocabulary Bank

| Category | Recurring Words/Phrases |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Texture** | 촉촉한 (dewy), 매끈한 (smooth), 가벼운 (light), 얇고 가볍게 밀착 (thin & light adhesion) |
| **Glow** | 물광 (water-glow), 광채 (radiance), 맑은 (clear), 투명한 (transparent), 빛나는 (luminous) |
| **Feeling** | 편안한 (comfortable), 부드러운 (gentle), 건강한 (healthy), 자연스러운 (natural) |
| **Action** | 채워주다 (fill/replenish), 되찾다 (restore), 깨우다 (awaken), 스며들다 (absorb/seep in) |
| **Positioning** | 데일리 (daily), 시그니처 (signature), 프리미엄 (premium), 히어로 (hero) |

### 3.3 Emoji System
Emojis are **structural**, not decorative. They serve as visual anchors:

| Emoji | Assigned Meaning |
|-------|-----------------|
| ✨ | Glow, radiance, transformation — the universal closer |
| 💎 | Premium quality, firmness, luxury |
| 💧 | Hydration, moisture |
| 🖤 | Elegance, premium line items |
| 🩵 | Hydrogel / water-based products |
| 💗 / 💖 | Pink line products, affection |
| 🌿 / 🌱 | Natural ingredients, CICA |
| 🧬 | Science-backed / biotech ingredients |
| ✔ / ✔️ | Functional benefit checkmarks |
| ❄️ | Winter / seasonal skincare |
| 🤍 | Soft elegance, closure |

### 3.4 Rhetorical Devices

**Contrast framing** — Position the product by contrasting two approaches:
> "Not just moisturizing — recovery and protection, too"
> "Not looking pale — looking genuinely radiant"

**Sensory storytelling** — Describe texture and experience before features:
> "Melting slowly into the skin like it dissolves… moisture gently wraps up your tired day"

**Ingredient-to-feeling translation** — Always bridge science to emotion:
> "CICA soothes tired skin gently, then PDRN fills it with bouncy energy from within 🌱🧬"

**Conversational questions** — Create a dialogue rhythm:
> "Does your skin get sensitive every winter?"
> "Still just moisturizing and stopping there?"

**Minimalist aspiration** — "Less is more" philosophy:
> "Beautiful skin doesn't have to be complicated 🌿"
> "Simple but comfortable skincare routine"
> "Not heavy coverage — just making your skin look more beautiful"

### 3.5 AI Disclosure Pattern
When AI-generated imagery or content is used, a standard disclaimer is appended before the hashtag block:

```
이 콘텐츠는 AI에 의해 생성되었습니다.
```
or
```
*해당 콘텐츠는 AI 기술을 활용하여 제작되었습니다.
```

## 5. CONTENT PILLARS

Based on the analyzed content library, LUBYLAB social content rotates across **4 pillars**:

| Pillar | Description | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Skin Scenario** | Opens with a relatable skin problem (dryness, dullness, sensitivity) and introduces the product as the answer | ~40% |
| **Lifestyle Moment** | Frames the product within a daily life context (morning routine, before a date, after work) | ~25% |
| **Ingredient Education** | Highlights a key ingredient (NAD+, PDRN, CICA, Glutathione, Spicule) and explains its benefit conversationally | ~20% |
| **Aesthetic / Mood** | Minimal text, sensory descriptions, emphasis on feeling and visual beauty | ~15% |
